Industry Projects

We take on projects from companies with the prupose of developing trading strategies or models pertaining to financial risk. Below are current projects we are working on.

The Mathematical Investment Research Council was approached by Bearing Capital Partners to develop and implement a strategy for locking in/paying out insurance on volatility using exchange traded instruments. The strategy involves a model which finds the optimal combination of various indicators related to the behaviour of uncertain markets and the instruments traded.

Personal Projects

We develop our own financial models and trading algorithms to aid us in investments and competitions such as the CME Trading challenge. All projects are showcased at council meetings.

In January of 2017, the MIRC had come across changepoint analysis which is a method for detecting changes in time series models. The CATA/Mk-1 uses simple buy low, sell high trading strategy but with a twist. The changepoint method tracks which state the stock/ETF is in. As long as the ETF/stock is in the state the algorithm will trade. However if a changepoint is detected then the state has changed and the algorithm will clear the ETF/stock off the portfolio. It will resume trading once the new state has been established. The CATA/Mk-1 has a Sharpe ratio of 2.8, and a 380% ROI over 3 years. Future improvements in CATA/Mk-2 will allow it to track and read news articles using a neural net and eliminate any drawdown effects.
